CSK Through the Years

From inception to dynasty

2008

The Beginning - IPL Inception

Chennai Super Kings were founded as one of the original eight IPL franchises. MS Dhoni was appointed captain, and Stephen Fleming as head coach, marking the start of one of cricket's most successful partnerships. Despite a strong debut season, CSK finished as runners-up, losing to Rajasthan Royals in the final.

MS Dhoni Appointed Captain Runners-up
2009

Semi-Final Heartbreak in South Africa

The IPL moved to South Africa due to general elections in India. CSK finished second in the league stage with 17 points and qualified for the semi-finals. Matthew Hayden was sensational, winning the Orange Cap with 572 runs at a strike rate of 145. However, they lost to Royal Challengers Bangalore by 6 wickets in the semi-finals. Stephen Fleming officially took over as head coach this season.

Semi-Finalists Hayden - Orange Cap (572 runs) Fleming as Coach
2010
üèÜ

First Championship Glory

CSK claimed their maiden IPL title, defeating Mumbai Indians by 22 runs in the final. Suresh Raina was exceptional throughout the tournament, accumulating 520 runs. This victory also marked the beginning of CSK's dominance in the tournament and established them as the team to beat.

IPL Champions CLT20 Winners Suresh Raina - Orange Cap
2011
üèÜ

Back-to-Back Champions

CSK successfully defended their title, beating Royal Challengers Bangalore by 58 runs in the final. This victory cemented their status as the most consistent team in the IPL. MS Dhoni's captaincy and the team's balanced approach were key to their success.

IPL Champions Second Consecutive Title
2013

The Scandal Year

Despite reaching the finals and performing well on the field, CSK was embroiled in the IPL betting scandal. Team officials were found guilty, which would later lead to significant consequences for the franchise. On the field, they lost to Mumbai Indians in a thrilling final.

Runners-up Betting Scandal
2015

Final Season Before Suspension

CSK qualified for the playoffs but were eliminated in the Qualifier 2. This would be their last season for two years as the consequences of the 2013 scandal finally caught up with the franchise.

Playoffs Last Season
2016-17

The Dark Years - Suspension

Chennai Super Kings were suspended from the IPL for two years due to their owners' involvement in the 2013 betting scandal. The Yellow Army waited patiently for their team's return, with the slogan "Whistle Podu" echoing louder than ever in anticipation.

2-Year Ban Fan Loyalty Peak
2018
üèÜ

The Comeback Kings

In one of the greatest comeback stories in sports, CSK returned from their two-year ban and immediately won the IPL title, defeating Sunrisers Hyderabad in the final. Shane Watson's incredible century in the final is considered one of the greatest IPL innings ever. This victory proved that CSK's winning mentality remained intact.

IPL Champions Watson's 117* Triumphant Return
2019

Heartbreak in the Final

CSK reached their eighth IPL final but fell short by just one run against Mumbai Indians in one of the most thrilling finishes in IPL history. Lasith Malinga's final over heroics denied CSK a fourth title.

Runners-up Lost by 1 run
2021
üèÜ

Fourth Title in Dubai

Despite the challenges of playing in UAE due to the pandemic, CSK claimed their fourth IPL title by defeating Kolkata Knight Riders in the final. Faf du Plessis and Ruturaj Gaikwad formed one of the most formidable opening partnerships in the tournament.

IPL Champions UAE Triumph Gaikwad - Orange Cap
2023
üèÜ

Fifth Title - Joint Most Successful

CSK won their fifth IPL title, defeating Gujarat Titans in a rain-affected final. This victory tied them with Mumbai Indians as the most successful franchise in IPL history. MS Dhoni's leadership in what many believed could be his final season made the victory even more special.

IPL Champions (5th) Joint-Most Titles Dhoni's Masterclass
2024

New Era Begins - Captaincy Handed to Gaikwad

MS Dhoni handed over the captaincy to Ruturaj Gaikwad just before the season began, marking the end of his 16-year captaincy reign. However, CSK struggled under new leadership, finishing 5th with 7 wins and 7 losses, failing to qualify for the playoffs for the first time since 2020. This marked a period of transition and rebuilding for the franchise.

New Captain: Ruturaj Gaikwad 5th Place Missed Playoffs
2025

Dhoni Returns as Captain

When Ruturaj Gaikwad was ruled out mid-season due to an elbow injury, MS Dhoni made a surprising return as captain. The veteran leader once again took the reins in what could be one of his final seasons, showing that CSK's faith in their talismanic captain remains unwavering. The squad retained key players including Jadeja, Pathirana, and Shivam Dube.

Dhoni's Comeback Mid-Season Change Season in Progress
